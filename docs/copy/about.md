## Why Kamala exists

There are a lot of weekends being sold as wellness. Most of them are holidays in soft lighting.

Kamala is something else. We built it for the moments in between — the seasons of life that ask you to slow down and pay attention. The grief you haven't sat with. The decision you've been avoiding. The version of you that's quietly asking to be let through.

We hold three days. We feed you well. We bring in practitioners who know what they're doing. The rest is yours.

## Who it's for

You're tired in a way sleep doesn't fix.

You've done some inner work and you're ready to do more, in a room with people who won't flinch.

You want time without your phone, without alcohol, without small talk.

You're willing to be uncomfortable for a weekend if it means something shifts.

You don't need a full week. You need three days held properly.

## Who it's not for

This isn't a spa break. There's a spa, and you'll use it, but that's not the point.

It isn't a party. There's no alcohol. The lights go down at ten.

It isn't a silent retreat or a religious retreat. We sit, we eat, we talk, we cry sometimes. There's structure, and there's space.

It isn't for people who want to be entertained. The weekend asks you to participate.

If you're in acute crisis or actively unwell, this isn't the right setting. Please look after yourself first and come to us when you're ready.

## What you can expect

Small groups. We cap numbers deliberately so the work stays intimate.

Curated practitioners. Every modality on the schedule has been chosen for how it sits with the rest of the weekend.

Real food. Seasonal, generous, dairy free, gluten free, nut free across the board.

Private estates. We hire whole houses so the group has the space to itself.

Honest holding. We don't oversell, we don't perform, and we don't pretend the weekend will fix you. It won't. But it might give you the room to start.
