# The Kamala philosophy

## Subtitle / dek
Unapologetically choosing yourself.

## The manifesto

Unapologetically choosing yourself. Taking the time, and the courage, to rise through the murky waters. Letting the world bask in awe at the very best version of you — not because you performed it, but because you finally let it through.

We navigate beautifully humbling hurdles. We move through ebbs and flows we didn't choose. The work is to reflect, to appreciate, to celebrate the journey as it actually is — not as we wish it had been.

And in that acceptance, everything changes.

## Trust the process

Most of the time, the process doesn't look like progress.

It looks like sitting with something you'd rather not sit with. It looks like a journal entry that goes nowhere. It looks like crying in a room full of strangers and not knowing why.

Trusting the process means showing up to the next thing on the schedule even when you can't see the point yet. It means letting the weekend do what weekends like this do. It means not needing the answer before the question has finished forming.

You don't have to believe in any of it. You just have to keep showing up.

## Get comfortable with being uncomfortable

Discomfort is information.

The breath that catches in your chest during somatic work. The tightness in your jaw during the gong. The thing you almost said at dinner and didn't. These aren't problems to fix — they're places the body has been holding something on your behalf, sometimes for years.

We're not interested in pushing past discomfort. We're interested in sitting near it. Long enough that it can speak. Long enough that something in you can answer back.

The weekend is held so that you can do this safely. With practitioners who know what they're doing. In a room where nobody is watching, and nobody is asking you to be okay.

## Do the work

There's no version of this where the work gets done for you.

We can hold the cacao, light the candles, set the room. We can bring you food that nourishes you and music that moves you. We can put a sound healer at the edge of the pool while you float.

But the work — the actual rising — is yours.

It's the choice to put your phone away. To say the thing you came here to say. To let the breath go where it wants to go. To stop performing, even just for the weekend, even just for an hour.

This is where we grow.
This is where we rise.
This is where we glow.

This is Kamala.

If something in this is calling you, come.
